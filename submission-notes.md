### Abstract (in case one is requested)

Letters don't normally carry an abstract, but if one is needed:

> Gibson (2026) argues that dependency grammar is the simplest theory of grammar, with dependency locality as the unifying principle behind processing effects and word-order universals. The representational case and the locality evidence are both strong. This Letter argues that the broader title claim overreaches. Three dissociations press against a single-factor story: licensed meanings that the same dependency skeleton cannot distinguish; differential satiation across English islands, with the Left Branch Condition as the stubborn exception; and agreement-attraction effects that concentrate in ungrammatical strings. Gibson's own concluding formulation, that dependency grammar is the simplest formalism for how words are connected within a sentence, is the narrower claim the evidence supports. Grammaticality asks more than whether the arrows can be drawn.

(149 words.)
